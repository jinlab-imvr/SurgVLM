Abstract

We propose SurgiX, a surgical foundation model trained on SurgDB, a large-scale multimodal surgical dataset comprising over 30 million samples across 10+ surgical tasks. SurgDB unifies and refines data from 20+ public datasets, with standardized labels and hierarchical instruction alignment to facilitate both low-level visual understanding and high-level procedural reasoning. SurgiX is built upon Qwen2.5, and undergoes multimodal projector pretraining to align visual-text representations, followed by image-level and video-level instruction tuning to capture spatial and temporal surgical dynamics. Comprehensive evaluations show that the proposed SurgiX consistently surpasses state-of-the-art general-purpose vision-Language models (e.g., GPT-4o, Gemini 2.0 Flash, Qwen2.5-Max) across six diverse surgical benchmarks.SurgiX achieves 96.5% relative improvement on phase recognition, 87.7% on action recognition, 150.0% on triplet prediction, 28.9% on critical view safety detection, 59.4% on visual question answering, and 198.5% on instrument localization. Beyond raw gains, SurgiX demonstrates robust generalization and open‑vocabulary QA, establishing a scalable, accurate, and clinically reliable paradigm for unified surgical intelligence.
